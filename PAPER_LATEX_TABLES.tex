\begin{comment}
Table~\ref{tab:ocr-comparison} presents the performance comparison of seven OCR models 
on the Malaysian receipt dataset. Claude 4.5 Sonnet achieved the highest accuracy at 
78.87\%, representing a 39.70\% improvement over the Tesseract baseline (p < 0.001). 
The model also demonstrated superior performance in Character Error Rate (CER: 21.13\%) 
and layout preservation (85.0 out of 100). However, this came at the cost of processing 
speed, being 39.3× slower than Tesseract.

Among the tested models, Qwen VL Plus offered the best balance between accuracy and 
speed, achieving 66.94\% accuracy while processing images only 9.8× slower than Tesseract. 
Notably, Qwen VL Plus also achieved the highest Entity F1 score (69.50\%), indicating 
superior performance in extracting structured information such as dates, prices, and 
invoice numbers.

All vision-language models demonstrated statistically significant improvements over 
Tesseract (Table~\ref{tab:improvement}), with accuracy gains ranging from 15.49\% 
to 39.70\% (all p < 0.05). The reduction in error rates was particularly pronounced, 
with CER improvements of 20-51\% and WER improvements of 57-76\%.
\end{comment}
